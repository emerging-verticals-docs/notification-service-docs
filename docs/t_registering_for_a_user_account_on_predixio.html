Registering for a Predix Platform User Account

When you register for a Predix account, a Cloud Foundry user account is created and you are given a Cloud Foundry org and space.

An individual account gives you one org and one space within that org. An Enterprise account gives you one org with one or more spaces. Individual users can add additional spaces to an org assigned to them. Enterprise users can add additional users and spaces to the org assigned to them using the Predix.io user interface.

  1. To register as an individual user, go to http://predix.io and click Free Trial.
    If you are an enterprise user, contact Predix Customer Support at gedigital@ge.com for help setting up your account.
  2. Enter your information on the form and click Continue Registration.
  3. Enter your physical business or personal address and click Verify Location.

    When you register, you are assigned a Cloud Foundry domain (<API_Endpoint>). Depending on your Predix.io registration, the value of <API_Endpoint> is one of the following:

    • Predix US-West

      https://api.system.aws-usw02-pr.ice.predix.io

    • Predix US-East

      https://api.system.asv-pr.ice.predix.io

    • Predix Japan

      https://api.system.aws-jp01-pr.ice.predix.io

    • Predix UK

      https://api.system.dc-uk01-pr.ice.predix.io

  4. Sign into Predix.io using the email and password you provided during registration.

    The org and spaces assigned to you are listed on the Console page. Your user type dictates your org and space assignments:

    User Type Org Name Default Space Name Description
    Individual Email address specified by the user during registration. dev An individual account provides you with one org and one space within that org. If you have an individual account, you cannot add users to your org.
    Enterprise Company name specified by the user during registration. dev An Enterprise account provides you with one org and one or more spaces. Enterprise accounts allow you to create teams by inviting team members to be part of the org, and then adding them to a space.
